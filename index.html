Recipes

Ichiban Dashi

Ingredients
  • 1.5 liters of water
  • 10 grams of Kombu (Dried Kelp)
  • 40 grams of Katsuobushi (Bonito Flakes)
  1. Make small cuts around the kelp and place in pot of water. This will allow more surface area to flavor the water.
  2. (Optional) Leave pot of water and kelp in refridgerator overnight.
  3. Bring water and kelp to a slow boil on Medium heat.
  4. Scoop out the scum while heating.
  5. Once the water starts to boil a bit, turn off flame and remove the Kombu. Save for Niban Dashi.
  6. Place the katsuobushi in the pot and let steep for 2 minutes
  7. Use a cheesecloth or paper towels and a strainer and dump dashi into a bowl straining the katsuobushi. Save for Niban Dashi.
  8. Done!

Niban Dashi

Ingredients
  • 1 liter of water
  • Leftover kombu and katsuobushi from ishiban dashi
  • 5 grams of Katsuobushi (Bonito Flakes)
  1. Place 1 liter of water with the leftover kombu and katusobushi in a pot.
  2. Bring to a boil.
  3. Once it starts boiling, turn down the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Scoop out the scum while simmering.
  5. After 10 minutes, turn off heat and place the fresh 5 grams of katsuobushi in the pot.
  6. Steep for 2 minutes and strain into a bowl.
  7. Done!
